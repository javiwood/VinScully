Vin Scully
Legendary Broadcaster

A Brief Timeline of Vin Scully’s Accomplishments:

  • 1927 – Born in the Bronx, New York.
  • 1950 – Started broadcasting for the Brooklyn Dodgers.
  • 1953 – At 25, became the youngest person to broadcast a World Series game (a record that still stands).
  • 1957 – Dodgers moved to Los Angeles, and Scully continued as their iconic voice.
  • 1976 – Voted the "most memorable personality" in franchise history by the fans.
  • 2016 – After 66 years, Scully announced his retirement. His final broadcast was in October, covering the Dodgers vs. Giants game.
